About Ladies’ Night

About Ladies’ Night

Ladies’ Night is a quarterly happy hour for the digitally-minded women, committed to conversation, collaboration, and community. Ladies’ Night was founded in 2014 and is an on-going effort by Mary Blalock, Ginger Craft, Stefanie Hatcher, and Jordan A. Smith. If you would like to sponsor or volunteer to help Ladies’ Night, please get in touch.

About Struck

About Struck

Struck is a creative agency (with offices in Portland, Salt Lake City and Los Angeles) focused on helping clients defy logic, overcome obstacles and outpace competition—through strategic insights and breakthrough creativity. The agency develops powerful brand solutions and experiences for clients such as Jack in the Box, Nickelodeon, The Weather Channel, Utah Office of Tourism, Experience Scottsdale, Creminelli and Universal Studios.

Code Of Conduct

Code Of Conduct

We don’t shine, if you don’t shine.

Ladies’ Night is an event series platform that is dedicated to inclusive conversation, collaboration and community in a safe space. It is our goal to provide women of all backgrounds (regardless of gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, ability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, age, religion, or socioeconomic status) an ability to come together to share their experiences with each other. In order to maintain this level of environment, we have a code of conduct that we hold our founders, volunteers, and all attendees to. We are an entirely harassment-free environment.

Harassment includes any behavior and/or language that is offensive to anyone’s gender, sexual preference, race, religion, physical appearance, disability, unwanted physical contact, or deliberate intimidation.

Every attendee of a Ladies’ Night affiliated event or member of any of our social channels is expected to maintain this level of conduct in all their interactions.

Reporting & Enforcing

If you or someone you know is being harassed please reach out to hello@ladiespdx.com

All reports will be handled confidentially and to the appropriate degree in order for the harassed to feel safe again.

Please find Ginger, Mary, Stefanie, or Jordan at a Ladies’ Night event if you have any questions.

PDX UTA

UTA is dedicated to increasing the representation of Blacks, Latinxs, Natives and other underrepresented races in the Portland tech scene through education, volunteer work and social events. UTA believes the tech industry, communities of color, and the country as a whole would benefit from the inclusion of all backgrounds in the tech sector.
